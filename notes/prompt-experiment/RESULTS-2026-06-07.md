# Composer Prompt A/B Experiment — Results (2026-06-07)

**Question:** Does restructuring the composer system prompt (not changing the
model) lift the cheap model's (`gpt-5.4-mini`) vague_term recall — and which
structural lever does it? Run as a clean single-variable experiment after the
operator paused the advisor-checkpoint work (which would otherwise confound it).

**Method (two-tier):**
- **Compose-only screen** (N=15/variant): stop after the composer drafts +
  stages review cards (Turn 2b), before execution. Captures the primary metric
  (vague_term under-flag) at ~47s/run → high N. Added behind `HARNESS_COMPOSE_ONLY=1`.
- **Full-battery confirm** (N=8, extremes V0 & B): real tutorial path incl.
  execution → ground-truth under-flag + over-flag + execution-validation + graduation.
- Model pinned `gpt-5.4-mini` throughout; each variant's `composer_skill_hash`
  stamped per run (swaps hash-verified: V0 `a617d0fe`, A `81fb8f47`, B `ccf3c78d`).

## Results

| Method | V0 baseline | A tightened | B restructured |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Full battery** (ground truth, N=8) | **75%** (6/8) | — | **38%** (3/8) |
| Compose-only screen (N=15) | 87% (13/15) | 73% (11/15) | 53% (8/15) |
| **SURGICAL (placement, shipped)** compose-only N=15 | — | — | **40% (6/15)** |
| over-flag (all 61 runs) | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| exec-fail (full N=8) | 6/8 | — | 7/8 |
| graduated (full N=8) | 2/8 | — | 1/8 |

## Conclusions (all three pre-registered hypotheses resolved)

- **H1 (primary) — CONFIRMED.** Restructuring **roughly halves** the mini's
  vague_term under-flag: full battery 75%→38%; compose-only 87%→53%. Both methods
  independently agree on a ~50% relative reduction. The operator's hypothesis was
  right: the prompt — not just model capability — was a major driver of the mini's
  vague_term misses.
- **Lever isolation:** the compose-only ladder (V0 87% → A 73% → B 53%) shows
  **placement** (B-vs-A, hoisting vague_term out of the dead-center + the
  Operating Contract + end checklist) contributed more than **conciseness alone**
  (V0-vs-A). The lost-in-the-middle fix is the dominant lever.
- **H2 (control) — CONFIRMED.** Execution-validation stayed bad under both prompts
  (V0 6/8, B 7/8 exec-fail). Restructuring does NOT touch it — that class is
  reasoning-bound (the A,B,C-vs-K,L,M field-contract mismatch a small model can't
  reconcile), owned by the deterministic field-contract guard in
  `elspeth-dac6602a2b`, not the prompt.
- **H3 (guard) — CONFIRMED.** Over-flag stayed **0 across all 61 runs**. No variant
  bought recall by introducing false positives — the operator's false-negative-only
  doctrine is respected.

## Honest caveats

- **The baseline is genuinely bad (~75–87%), not the rosy 33% from the earlier
  N=6** (that was small-sample noise — the same trap that briefly showed B at 2/3
  mid-run before settling to 3/8). The mini under-fires vague_term on ~3 of 4
  baseline tutorial runs.
- **B helps a lot but does not SOLVE it.** 38% residual under-flag on the mini is
  still far from the operator's ~99% target. Prompt restructure is ONE lever, not
  the whole answer. To reach ~99% on the cheap path, combine B with the advisor
  checkpoints (`dac6602a2b`, paused) and/or the tutorial-scoped stronger model
  (opus hit 0/6 under-flag — a strong model eliminates it). The levers are now
  quantified and complementary.
- **Power:** the full-battery N=8 alone is underpowered (V0 6/8 vs B 3/8, Fisher
  p≈0.3). The credibility comes from **convergence**: the powered compose-only
  screen (13/15 vs 8/15, p≈0.05) AND the full battery agree on direction AND
  magnitude (~halving). Neither alone is conclusive; together they are.

## What shipped (LOCKED IN — commit b85d29696, release/0.5.3, 2026-06-07)

Promotion of variant B verbatim broke 3 production contracts (10 content-backstop
tests pinning exact rule-strings, the AUTOGEN tool-inventory block, the
advisor-strip heading). 9 of the 10 backstops were rules B *preserved but
reworded*; 1 was intentionally-suppressed prompt-shield content. Rather than
rewrite those backstops, we applied the **dominant validated lever — placement —
surgically** to the baseline: an "Operating Contract" at the top (the 4
non-negotiable rules incl. the vague_term authoring trigger) + a copy-this
termination checklist at the end. **Additive only**, so all 85 content-contract
tests + 473 composer unit tests stay green (provably no rule dropped), AUTOGEN and
advisor-strip untouched.

**Result: the surgical version BEAT full B** — 40% under-flag (6/15) vs B's 53%,
vs baseline 87% (Fisher p≈0.01 vs baseline), over-flag 0. Likely because it keeps
*both* the prominent top trigger AND the full body mechanics, where B condensed
the body and lost signal. The cautious, backstop-preserving path was also the more
effective one.

## Original recommendation (superseded by "What shipped" above)

1. ~~Promote variant B~~ → superseded: surgical placement shipped instead
   (outperformed B, zero backstop churn). B remains a reference artifact in
   `notes/prompt-experiment/`.
2. **Do NOT expect B to fix execution-validation** — that is `dac6602a2b`'s
   deterministic field-contract guard + the model lever.
3. **Cheap-path recall target** = B (prompt) + advisor checkpoints + (optionally)
   a tutorial-scoped stronger model. Tutorial path: a stronger model alone already
   hits 0/6.
